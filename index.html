🔬 What is Quantum Computing?

Regular computers use bits — like tiny light switches that are either ON or OFF. Quantum computers use qubits, which can be ON, OFF, or both at the same time thanks to a weird quantum trick called superposition! This lets quantum computers solve some problems millions of times faster than regular computers. Scientists at DARPA and companies like Tetrabolt LLC are working on quantum technology every day!

🪙 Quantum Coin Flip

Heads: 0  |  Tails: 0

🧠 What's happening?

Classical coin = always 1 state (heads OR tails), even while spinning.
Quantum coin (qubit) = BOTH heads AND tails at the same time while spinning — until you look at it! The moment you measure it, it "chooses" one. This magical both-at-once state is called superposition. It's how quantum computers try all answers at the same time!

🧠 What's happening?

Einstein called this "spooky action at a distance." When two particles are entangled, changing one instantly affects its partner — no matter how far apart they are! Even if Bob is on Mars (225 million km away), the moment Alice changes, Bob responds instantly. Scientists have proven this is real! This is used in quantum cryptography to make communication that is impossible to spy on.

🧠 What's happening?

A classical computer is like one explorer trying every path one at a time — slow! A quantum computer is like having thousands of explorers trying ALL paths at the same time. The moment any explorer reaches the exit, the quantum computer is done! This is called quantum parallelism, and it's why quantum computers can solve mazes (and much harder problems) incredibly fast.
